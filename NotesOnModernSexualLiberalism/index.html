Notes on Modern Sexual Liberalism

Ioannes Šubić

I. Corruption.

    The sexual liberation of the commons during the late 60’s into the early 70’s caused many riffs within society. The cult of the body erected by the runners of the social revolution had completely backfired. Much like leprosy, the sickness that is human aging is slow and necrotic. Slowly becoming old and rotten themselves, emancipation of the body was left behind in the dreams of their youth. What the young revolutionaries did not see was the conformity of capital. Capital cannibalizes every factor of human life. The initial slogans cried during May of 68’ and following months overseas in America were trailed by acceptance and commercialization. Hedonistic free love movements spread among the young, financially free generation, extending the political realm into the sexual. While much good came of this, notably the beginning inclusion and normalization of LGBTQ+ rights, much in the realm of the body and beauty had been corrupted.     Generally, the pursuit of sexual freedom was constructed by the superstructure of their time, a larger distraction from true emancipation. The legacy has been bitter for culture at large.1 Normalization of pornography had skyrocketed in the early 70’s, leading to capitalization of film of sex. While initially revolutionary in the fight against government censorship laws of the late 60’s, quickly the film movement was co-opted by the mainstream wings of Hollywood and European cinema to be capitalized on. After the wide stream acceptance of video tape in the 80’s, the erotic film scene had been demoted from preformative art to cheap mass produced smut. The embers of institutional change were extinguished. Many industries followed suit, plastic surgery exploded, selective surgeries like liposuction and implants quickly joined the scene, and idealized images were pushed on magazines, television, and radio, encouraging an idealized temple of body. While the new chic during the early days of the revolution, they would suction to the gut of society like leeches, influencing body trends to today. It has left a major refraction from the ideals of the movement. The temple of the body was desecrated, forced through surgery and disorder to strive for unrealistic standards. Hypersexuality has become a virtue, sexuality without love is seen as normal. One questions what the movement would look like today if not sidelined by the young generation who had started it. Surely it could not be worse than what is current. The edge of the 21st century has shown the great repercussions the movement has had on the mental health of the young generation, with no view of change in sight.

II. Love and Narcissism.

    There is a modern notion of love for the world, love for everyone, egalitarian love. That everyone is equal socially within the eyes of the beholder. This rose-tinted posturing drapes the real truth of the nature of love. Love is not a blanketed act distributed to everyone. It is not designated by fate of supernatural origin towards every individual. It is inherently a situational evil in this way. It is the idea that another would selfishly designate their inner object of desire to another being. It is a wholehearted rejection of the collective, dividing and caring for the singular. That is why love is a beautifully evil act. Slavoj Žižek says that love is within the “structure of imbalance.” It discriminates, it is tribal, it is not egalitarian. It does not serve as a term for greater blanketed acceptance and enjoyment of everything. This is another core concept that the free love movement had miscalculated. The slogans of free love and Jouissez sans entraves (“Enjoy without hindrance”) empowered the new self-centred, financially free generation to expand and experiment with the definition of love. Young narcissistic baby boomers vying for new sexual experiences opened the road for not a liberation of the culture of sex and love, but the commodification of it. Revolutionary potential slowly backslided into new-age fads and now is everywhere and inescapable. It is as much forged into the identity of every young boy and girl as language. Implemented into the very growth of the next generation, exposed at a young age. Sexual appeal has become another road for both social and economic advancement underneath capital, another road for oppression. Society is forced to crawl through it, for the rest of its declining existence. It’s a burden everyone jointly holds on their back, willingly or not, though mostly willingly.     In the novel Whatever, author Michel Houellebecq quite vulgarly describes the cryptic perverted culture in which his character rightly sees the world through. Everything is dirty, libidinal, and cynical. I emphasize rightly because modern society is a clearly perverted culture. Capital uproots the neat lines of culture and controls their use for monetary gain. It is an inherently sexual act. Much like love is the selfish singling out of the individual from the whole, the sexual nature of the economy is the self-sustaining accumulative narcissistic act, desiring the whole for one, and co-opting new movements and trends, ripping them from their origins and grooming them towards commodification. The true horror is the realization that the superstructure is larger than what is imaginable within local communities. In a globalized world, it is a Goliath, a hegemonic god-king that cannot be dethroned from its post. Participants in the structure validate its rule. From rich to poor, attractive to unsightly, black to white, the system is upheld even in the cases where it should be protested. Much like the weak leper accepting his spot as servant to the priest’s code of man, the populace subscribes to its own oppressor hoping to find their time in its excessive material glory.     The narcissism valued in the society subconsciously forces many to forgo answering valuable questions to their individual state. This may take the form of repressed insecurities, avoiding crucial periods in the development of life in search of societal elevation (Working shifts from a young age, Dropping out of school to join the workforce), or ostracism of the needy or declining. It not only favours individual industry achievements at the behest of one’s life, but the punishment of those who have less to offer. It is in this way that the spirit of capital contorts the message of natural love into narcissism. It paints natural love into the free love of the 70’s, “Be hedonistic, Love everyone off the assumption they will exchange this love back to you” while carrying the underlying message that love is to be exploited in the same way people have been, it is for only the individual’s benefit. Love should return to the individually selfish but ultimately selfless act that it is, one of love not for profit, but for imbalance and human connection, one not based on returns or individual gain, but one of passion and desire.

III. Internalization

    How does the overarching structural corruption of the new sexual culture affect the singular? Clearly the question is hard to answer with a definite constitution. On the other hand there are clearly rigid underlying factors of the new sexual culture which affect every single person. Previously outlined, the sexual-liberal system does not have use for ill-confident, misshapen, commodities. It has an extremely primal way of division, one which makes those that perceive themselves as not tough enough to tread on the water decline rapidly. I suggest that it has led to a greater social disruption than admitted by the public. Mental disorders have been on a steep rise for the current young generation. Some consider the generation as the “most lonely”, even in the modern interconnected world. Isolation is an extremely deep theme within the youth. The standard suggests that the corruption and self hate of the young is directed by media and product advertisements as an analysis seems naive. There is clearly a bigger issue at hand, as sex had not been completely devoid from the society that had come before the sexual revolution.     So what is the new influence? The problem with a capitalistic society which has adapted and contorted the values of a revolutionary sexual liberation movement is that the contortion has led to more than profit. It has led to a great social panic which has riffed the generation to come, only now slowly being accounted for. Many turn to therapy and psychology to help, trying to find pathological descriptions of their individual disease instead of probing the structure they live in. The problem with an idealized individualist society is that the onus is on the single to affirm themselves. They cannot accept any fault in the system for their failure, as it is seen as weakness and defeatism. It resembles a recursive process of self hate, snowballing and consuming its tail end to the point of self capitulation. Nothing is perfect enough for the system, you have lost if you don’t make enough money, don’t climb the social ladder fast enough, or aren’t pretty enough. The standards are not reachable. The structure becomes the state of being, as the only way out of the hole of self hatred is actualization and pattern breaking, which can only be done through accepting fault in the system which secures a perceived sense of safety. It is in this self referencing way that the system is monstrous and seems unbeatable.     There has been a large push in recent years to a proposed “solution” to the dilemma inflicted upon society’s psyche in the realm of sexual development. Often labelled the “body positive” movement, it focuses around an acceptance of “underrepresented bodies and encouragement of varying body types”. Of course one can easily critique the body positive movement as overly accepting of those with unhealthy and self-damaging lifestyles, but I do not see this as the main problem.2 Moreover, the body positive movement takes with it the same drawbacks that the sexual revolution had taken. Even within a system of perceived tolerance and acceptance of body diversity, the structure of capital’s tribalism takes root. The body positive movement does not search to liberate anything in the realm of the sexual, rather tolerate. Corporations have mopped the tendrils of the movement into a homogeneous value set no more genuine than the ads it is injected into. It has become another movement that has backslid into irrelevance, becoming another marketing ploy and standing for nothing concretely.     In reality, no new movement to liberate society from the chains of oppressive sexual liberalism and capitals grasp has formed. I believe it will not form in my lifetime. The system succeeds in the conscious awareness of the average citizen, and the problems are not vocalized enough to cause social change. We are stuck inside the machine, and it will bleed to death.
