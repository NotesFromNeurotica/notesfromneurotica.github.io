Thermadorian Mind (Sonnet)


Indecisive yet aggressive, It sits in bureaucratic deadlock; To it’s insecurities it is possessive, Forming excuses on inaction post hoc.

Inside there is desire to change, Fought by the fear of capitulation, And the anxiety to estrange; Brewing self-contempt begins it’s gestation.

It fights the meek and the prideful, Stabilizing on centre ground, Yet both wings do not stifle, The cabinet becomes unbound.

The psyche revolts in reactionary fervour, Towards the idealism of yesterday; Little has changed to an outer observer, But the emotional faculties have begun to decay.

Though the outcome of it’s brumaire is unclear, Destruction of the current state is astir.
